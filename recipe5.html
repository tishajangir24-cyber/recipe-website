Aloo Paratha

Golden, crispy, stuffed with love — Mom's Sunday morning special that we never get tired of.

⏱️ Prep: 20 min 🔥 Cook: 20 min ⏳ Total: 30 min 👥 Makes: 8 parathas 📊 Easy 🥦 Vegetarian

🛒 Ingredients

  • For the dough:
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • ¾ cup warm water (approx.)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil
  • For the filling:
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled & mashed
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter or ghee for cooking
  • Special ingredient :
  • Mom's love

👩‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Make the Dough: Mix wheat flour and salt. Add oil and gradually pour in warm water, kneading into a soft, smooth, pliable dough. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 20 minutes.
2
Prepare the Filling: Mash the boiled potatoes until completely lump-free (lumps will tear the dough!). Mix in green chilli, ginger, cumin, chilli powder, amchur, garam masala, coriander, and salt. Divide into 8 equal balls.
3
Stuff the Paratha: Divide the dough into 8 balls. Roll one ball into a 4-inch circle. Place a filling ball in the centre. Bring the dough edges together and pinch to seal completely. Flatten gently into a disc.
4
Roll Out: Dust the stuffed disc with dry flour. Roll gently into a 7–8 inch circle, applying even pressure. If the filling bursts through, patch with a bit of dough and keep rolling. Don't worry — it happens to everyone at first!
5
Cook the Paratha: Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium-high heat. Place the paratha on the dry tawa. After 1 minute, flip when small bubbles appear. Apply ghee or butter, spread it around. Flip again and apply more on the other side.
6
Finish and Serve: Press gently with a spatula and cook until both sides are golden brown with crispy spots, about 2–3 minutes per side. Serve immediately with cold yoghurt, pickle (achar), and a knob of butter on top. Heavenly!
